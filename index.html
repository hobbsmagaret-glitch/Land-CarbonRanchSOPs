Our Conviction

Land and Carbon operates on a single conviction: that the most degraded land holds the greatest potential, and that potential is unlocked not by fighting nature but by working within it.

The ranches we acquire are not broken — they are depleted. The soil biology is suppressed, the forage is diminished, the genetics are mismatched, and the economics reflect all of it. Our work is restoration: rebuilding the ecological engine beneath the grass so that everything above it — the animals, the carbon, the financial returns — compounds over time rather than declines.

We do this because we understand that biology precedes economics. Profit is not extracted from land; it is grown from it. Every management decision we make — how long the grass rests, which animals stay in the herd, whether we reach for a chemical or wait for adaptation — is an investment in a biological system that will either build or erode. We choose to build. That means our horizon is not the next sale or the next season. It is the next decade, and the one after that.

The world has no shortage of people willing to take from the land. We are building something rarer: an organization that knows how to give it back — and that understands, from the evidence of practitioners across four continents, that restoration and profitability are not in tension. They are the same project, measured over the right timeframe.

Grazing Management

Grazing Planning & Rotation Design

🗺️
  • ✓Core formula: Number of camps = Rest period (days) ÷ Occupation time per camp (days). Never cut rest periods short for convenience
  • ✓In high-quality forage environments use Long-Rest Continuous Rotation; in sourveld or warm-season dominant systems use Three-Zone Adaptive Grazing (TZAG)
  • ✓Distinguish overstocked (too many animals) from overgrazed (rest too short) — different problems, different fixes
  • ✓If rumen fill drops below 85% at day-start: increase camp size or move immediately
Grazing Management

Seasonal Grazing Management

🌦️
  • ✓Four annual phases: pre-season preparation → growing season → seasonal transition → non-growing season. Know which phase you're in
  • ✓Align calving window with the seasonal nutrition peak — this is your highest-leverage management decision of the year
  • ✓Seasonal transitions are highest-risk — enter the non-growing season with good BCS, adequate stockpile, and protein supplement in position
  • ✓Lengthen rest periods in the dry/dormant season; shorten cautiously during active peak growth only
Drought Response

Drought Management & Resilience

☀️
  • ✓Set your Body Condition Floor before drought arrives — when BCS hits the floor, destock or supplement regardless of market or sentiment
  • ✓Early destocking is a management response; late forced destocking is a financial crisis. Sell early — never wait
  • ✓Destock in order: first to sell are low-value, poor-adapted, or surplus animals; retain your best genetics at all costs
  • ✓After drought breaks: rest the land before restocking — do not rush animals back onto recovering pasture
Genetics

Animal Genetics & Breed Selection

🐄
  • ✓Three non-negotiable traits: inherent body condition (fatness gene), hormonal balance (sexual dimorphism), and calving ease (sloping rump)
  • ✓Select from "2+3 dams" — the dam's mother first calved at 2, then again at 3, unassisted on available forage. This is your fertility foundation
  • ✓Cull any cow requiring calving assistance, regardless of other performance — calving difficulty is a non-negotiable removal criterion
  • ✓Read animals in context: a thin cow on poor ground may be genetically superior to a fat cow on high inputs. Phenotype ≠ genotype
Livestock Nutrition

Livestock Nutrition

🌾
  • ✓Grass is the primary feed budget. Only supplement to bridge specific, identified gaps — not out of habit or "more is better" thinking
  • ✓Three field monitoring tools: rumen fill (check 3× daily), body condition score (daily when moving daily, monthly minimum otherwise), and fecal consistency — no lab required
  • ✓Protein supplement during the non-growing season when forage drops below rumen function threshold — this is the first and most important supplement
  • ✓Urea danger: fatal at >1g/kg body weight. Dilute carefully, never feed hungry animals, and never exceed label rates
Infrastructure

Infrastructure Development Standard

🏗️
  • ✓Genetics drive the timeline — infrastructure follows. The genetic shift (8–12 years) is the constraint, not the fencing or water systems
  • ✓Tier 1 (non-negotiable before cattle): water security and perimeter fence integrity. Never place cattle without these
  • ✓Tier 2 (Year 1): basic processing capability (weigh scale, AI chute) and the genetics programme running — these compound value from day one
  • ✓Virtual fencing (Vence/Halter) is compressing the infrastructure timeline — assess cell coverage before acquisition
Grazing Management

UHDG Principles & Setup

🌿
  • ✓Concentrate animals at very high density on small areas, move frequently — then allow extended rest and recovery before returning
  • ✓Monitor rumen fill 3× daily; record body condition score daily when moving cattle daily — condition trend is your primary decision-making guide, not a calendar
  • ✓Energiser voltage must exceed 3,000V at the furthest point — check daily; water infrastructure must be in place before starting
  • ✓A calm, purposefully grazing mob signals a well-functioning system; restlessness or fence-testing means investigate immediately
Framework — Grazing

Three-Zone Adaptive Grazing (TZAG)

🗂️
  • ✓For warm-season dominant and sourveld systems: divide property into Zone 1 (intensive UHDG during growing season), Zone 2 (seasonal buffer), Zone 3 (full rest + stockpile)
  • ✓Rotate zone assignments annually — no area stays in Zone 1 for consecutive years or it degrades from planned overgraze to genuine damage
  • ✓Zone 3 is your drought insurance. Always build it; never consume it early unless the alternative is forced destocking
  • ✓Reassign zone boundaries each year based on current forage condition, not fixed fences — best species composition areas go to Zone 1
